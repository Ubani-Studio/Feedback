# Tizita: The Honest Stress Test

## A commercial analyst's read on TAM, moat, ICP, naming, investors, allies, and the path to $1M ARR

---

## Summary

Tizita started as a photo organizer ("keep the best, delete the rest") and is morphing into the directorial cognition layer of an AI video pipeline.

This article gives the analyst's read:

- **Real TAM is smaller** than the "post-vanity creators globally" framing implies. Probably $750K to $6M ARR ceiling at manifesto-ICP saturation. Lifestyle business or small Series A, not venture-scale on that segment alone.
- **The actual moat is the orchestration layer**, not the cosmology. Cross-modal coherence, character continuity via Boveda, intelligent routing between Genoma / ComfyUI / ElevenLabs / Ibis / Slayt. The cosmology is brand differentiation, not product differentiation.
- **Near-term commercial play**: $25-40/mo creator subscription plus $200-500/mo studio API plus ComfyUI integration as the wedge. Path to $1M ARR in 18 months.
- **Naming**: keep **Tizita** if you have any brand traction (already deployed, beautiful word, defensible as adjacent-inheritance). Or rebrand to **Ikenga** (Igbo, on-substrate, manifesto-aligned, fits product semantics). Reject Hiraeth / Hiræth (cliche, wrong substrate), Xiræ / Xiræth (vanity coinage at no real bridge), Clarosa (already abandoned).
- **Investors**: Backed VC, Slow Ventures, MaC Venture, Concrete VC, TLcom Capital at seed. Index, Lightspeed, a16z Cultural Leadership Fund at Series A. Avoid metric-only seed funds.
- **Creator allies**: Hiro Murai, Kahlil Joseph, Akinola Davies Jr, Solange, Janelle Monáe, Childish Gambino, Steve McQueen, Kibwe Tavares.

In one line: **build for the commercial creator ICP, brand for the manifesto ICP, ship neutral defaults, win on integration, raise from culture-aware investors.**

## 1. The TAM stress test

The "post-vanity creators globally" framing inflates the addressable market. Real numbers, working from outside-in:

| Layer | Count |
|---|---|
| Global serious creators (full-time + serious side) | ~50M (Goldman, NeoReach 2024 estimates) |
| Conviction-driven / post-vanity subset | 5-10% = 2.5-5M |
| Diasporan / rupture-conscious subset (manifesto ICP) | 10-20% of above = 250K-1M |
| Subset who pay for niche creator tooling | 10-20% of above = 25K-200K |

At $30/mo subscription, fully saturating the manifesto ICP yields **$750K to $6M ARR ceiling**. That is a lifestyle business or a small Series A, not a venture-scale outcome.

Implication: the manifesto ICP is the **brand's truth-axis**, not the **product's TAM**. Build the product for a wider commercial creator ICP that overlaps with but exceeds the manifesto's "for-whom."

---

## 2. ICP refinement

**Primary commercial ICP** (where ARR comes from):

- Indie filmmakers and music video directors (~500K globally)
- Serious YouTube and TikTok creators with $100-500/mo software budget (~2M)
- Small production studios and creative agencies (~50K studios)
- AI video power users (ComfyUI, Krea, Runway power users, ~100K-500K)

Total primary commercial TAM: **~2.5-3M individual creators plus ~50K studios**.

**Aspirational / brand ICP** (where cultural differentiation comes from):

- Manifesto-aligned post-vanity creators (250K-1M as above)
- This is a SUBSET of the commercial ICP, not a separate market

The mistake is treating the brand ICP as the only ICP. The brand ICP is a flagship segment. The commercial ICP is the body. The brand ICP gives the product cultural credibility; the commercial ICP pays the bills.

---

## 3. Real moat audit

The cosmological vocabulary is **brand differentiation**, not product differentiation. Test: does it drive day-1 conversion or compound retention? No. It defends the brand against commoditization in a 3-5 year window. That is strategic, not commercial.

What actually moats the product:

| Moat | Strength | Time to defend |
|---|---|---|
| Cross-modal coherence (visual ↔ audio ↔ shot logic) | Strong | 18-24 months tech head start |
| Orchestration layer (Genoma / ComfyUI / ElevenLabs / Ibis / Slayt routing) | Very strong | Switching costs grow with usage |
| Character continuity via Boveda across renders | Strong | Solves real pain other tools punt on |
| Music-aware storyboard structure (scrubber, sections, intermittent gutter) | Medium | Demoable but copyable in 6-12 months |
| Cosmological depth (Subtaste, Manifesto-aligned vocabulary) | Brand-only | 3-5 year defensibility |
| Photo-first taste training | Weak | Other tools will catch up; not the moat |

**The integration moat is the commercial moat.** The cosmology is the cultural moat. Both matter, in that order, for the next 18 months.

## 5. Pricing and commercial play

Recommended structure:

- **Free tier**: 50 photos, 1 storyboard, no AI. Hooks taste training, demonstrates the cross-modal coherence value prop.
- **Creator tier**: $25-40/mo. Unlimited storyboards, full Boveda character library, Subtaste, ComfyUI export. Targets the 2-3M individual primary ICP.
- **Studio tier**: $200-500/mo. API access, character licensing, multi-seat. Targets the 50K studios and agencies.
- **Genoma render-credits resold** as second revenue stream, margin 15-25%.
- **Boveda character licensing** (long-term): a successful character lives across an artist's projects. License-per-use to other creators. Unproven; treat as upside, not core.

Path to $1M ARR in 18 months: ~3,000 paying creators at $30/mo average plus ~50 studio seats. Achievable with strong onboarding and ComfyUI distribution.

## 6. The naming decision

Candidates evaluated against three criteria:
(a) substrate alignment with the user's manifesto ground,
(b) brand defensibility and uniqueness,
(c) cost of switching from current state.

| Name | Substrate | Verdict |
|---|---|---|
| **Tizita** | Ethiopian / Amharic, "memory and longing" | Beautiful, in market, adjacent (East African) but not direct (Igbo / Ijaw / Jamaican / London) inheritance. **Defensible. Keep if any brand traction.** |
| **Hiraeth / Hiræth** | Welsh, "homesickness for what cannot return" | Cliche in artist circles, wrong substrate (Welsh is not your ground), heavily used in other products and writing. The Æ is typographic affectation. **Reject.** |
| **Xiræ / Xiræth** | Invented coinage | No bridge. Per Manifesto Article 4: *invention where none is needed is vanity*. The Æ is decoration, not coinage at a real crossing. **Reject.** |
| **Clarosa** | Spanish / Latinate, "clear rose" | Already abandoned in the codebase (clarosa.db remains as legacy filename only). No reason to revive. **Reject.** |
| **Ikenga** | Igbo, "achievement-figure / right-hand god of human endeavor" | On-substrate, manifesto-aligned, two syllables, strong sound. Perfect fit (a tool that helps you DO the work, the maker's right hand). **Strongest substrate-aligned alternative.** |
| **Afa** | Igbo, "divination / operational algebra" | Too short for trademark, hard to brand-search. **Reject for product.** |
| **Egbesu** | Ijaw, "god of righteous force" | Politically loaded (associated with Niger Delta militancy). Risk of brand entanglement. **Reject.** |
| **Mmanwu** | Igbo, "ancestor masquerade" | Unpronounceable for non-Igbo speakers. Adoption friction at first contact. **Reject.** |

### Recommendation

**If you have any meaningful brand traction with Tizita already** (URLs, social handles, codebase, mindshare in the ecosystem), **keep Tizita**. Frame it as adjacent-inheritance (East African, respectful borrowing, fits product semantics: memory and longing for what could be). Beautiful word, defensible. The manifesto explicitly endorses adjacent inheritance: "sister traditions and wider inheritance, adjacent grounds the method draws on and the work honours."

**If you are at a point where rebranding is cheap** (early enough that no one recognizes the name), **Ikenga** is the strongest manifesto-aligned alternative. Igbo, on-substrate, two syllables, strong sound, perfect product fit (the right hand that does the work, the achievement-figure, the maker's tool). Igbo-substrate-direct, not adjacent.

**Do not pick Hiraeth, Xiræth, Hiræth, or revive Clarosa.** Each fails for a specific reason (substrate mismatch, vanity coinage, abandonment, cliche).

## 9. The 12-month commercial play

**Months 1-3**: Ship ComfyUI integration. "Send to ComfyUI" with pre-loaded workflow JSON containing shot directives (palette tier, character refs, edit directive, music section). Onboarding flow that makes cross-modal coherence demoable in 30 seconds.

**Months 4-6**: Boveda character integration end-to-end. A character defined in Boveda flows to Tizita storyboards, to Ibis script, to ElevenLabs voice, to ComfyUI render. This is the spine of the product story. Each integration deepens the switching cost.

**Months 7-9**: Studio tier launch. API access, multi-seat, custom character licensing. First three studio customers as case studies.

**Months 10-12**: Music-video specific vertical (the scrubber, sections, intermittent gutter is genuinely unique). Partner with one or two Tier-2 directors for case studies. Press to NTS, Crack, Pitchfork, Resident Advisor.

**Goal**: $1M ARR by month 18, defensible integration moat established, manifesto-aligned brand voice in cultural press.

## 12. The honest summary

Tizita is two things at once:

1. **A commercial creator tool** that orchestrates AI video direction, with ~$1M ARR achievable in 18 months and a clear path to $5-10M in 3-4 years if the integration moat holds.
2. **The product manifestation of a manifesto-grade cultural project**, in which Cherubsphinx the artist deploys Boveda mapping across a body of work, with Tizita as one of the operative tools.

The risk is **collapsing layer 1 into layer 2** (overfit on the manifesto ICP, miss the commercial TAM) or **layer 2 into layer 1** (commodify the brand, lose the cultural moat).

The discipline is to keep them separate:

- **Commercial product** with neutral defaults, clean integrations, demoable features.
- **Manifesto-aligned brand voice** in press, About, artist-side communications.
- **Manifesto-rooted artist-world** that uses the product natively as a flagship case study.

Three layers, mutually reinforcing, never one eating the other.

That is the path.
