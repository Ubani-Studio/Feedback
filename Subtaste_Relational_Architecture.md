# Subtaste Relational Architecture

## Why 256 Beats 22, and Why the Rose Beats the Tree

---

## Summary

If Subtaste needs a relational grammar (how does glyph A stand against glyph B for tension, harmony, B-story compatibility), the candidates on the table are: Kenneth Grant's 22 Tunnels of Set (qliphothic Kabbalah), the dayside 22 paths of standard Kabbalah, Ifá's 256 Odù, Vodun's Rada/Petwo dialectic, and just-coin-it.

This article evaluates each, then recommends a stack:

1. **Engine layer**: Starforge's existing 4 modes (collaborate, tension, growth, avoid). Already in production. Already cross-modal. Don't rebuild.
2. **Geometry layer**: Subtaste Twelve as a Rose with six relational positions per pair (same, sextile, square, trine, quincunx, opposition). 12 × 6 = 72 pair-states. Cardinality fits.
3. **Inheritance axis**: Vodun's Rada (cool, inherited, patient) vs Petwo (hot, forged, new-world) as an orthogonal temperature axis cross-cutting every relation.
4. **Deep substrate**: Ifá's 256 Odù as the high-resolution oracle for canonization decisions and arc-spanning queries. Reserved, not default.
5. **Surface vocabulary**: relations named at the user's specific four-way crossroads (Igbo × Ijaw × Jamaican × London-diasporic), per Manifesto Article 4. Pluggable theme system in the product.
6. **Flavor layer**: Grant's tunnel names and Orisha dyads available as optional hover-labels per glyph-pair. Never default. Never grammar.

In one line: **the Rose, not the Tree. Petwo and Rada as the temperature axis. Ifá's 256 as the deep substrate when needed. Grant's tunnels are flavor only, never engine.**

---

## 1. What problem are we solving

Subtaste is a 12-glyph taste taxonomy. For storyboard B-story logic, a B-story works against an A-story in some defined relation: maximum tension, supportive resonance, awkward unresolved kinship. We need a vocabulary for these relations.

Three asks of the system:

1. **Cardinality fits 12.** A six-position relational geometry per pair gives 12 × 6 = 72 pair-states. Workable.
2. **Valence balanced.** Not every relation is "shadow" or "light." We need both registers.
3. **Culturally rooted.** Per the Boveda Mapping Manifesto, the grammar should not borrow Western typology when an indigenous substrate exists.

---

## 2. Kenneth Grant's 22 Tunnels of Set

Grant's *Nightside of Eden* (Skoob 1977/1994) maps 22 tunnels onto the qliphothic counterparts of the 22 paths of the Tree of Life. Each tunnel carries a name and a relational character:

> Amprodias, Baratchial, Gargophias, Dagdagiel, Hemethterith, Uriens, Zamradiel, Characith, Temphioth, Yamatu, Kurgasiax, Lafcursiax, Malkunofat, Niantiel, Saksaksalim, A'ano'nin, Parfaxitas, Tzuflifu, Qulielfi, Raflifu, Shalicu, Thantifaxath.

Each tunnel is a "nightside" path: descent, dissolution, formless inversion, vampyric reversal, etc.

**Strengths**:

- Rich qualitative vocabulary (each tunnel has a distinct character)
- Internally consistent system, derived from Crowley's *Liber 231* and Spare's *Atavistic Resurgence*
- Aesthetic gravity (the names alone do work)

**Limits**:

- **22 ≠ 12.** Cardinality mismatch with Subtaste. Cherry-picking 12 of the 22 loses the system's coherence (each tunnel's character comes from its position relative to the others on the Tree).
- **Single-valence.** Every tunnel is shadow. There is no equivalent "ascent" or "harmony" register in Grant's framework. A B-story relation system that only renders shadow biases the whole product toward darkness.
- **Cultural ownership.** Kabbalah is Jewish mystical tradition. Grant is one Western occult interpreter. Building a creative tool's relational engine on this borrows a tradition the user's manifesto explicitly refuses (Article 6: *Tree-thinking is colonial*).

---

## 3. The "light side" question

Is there a Grant-equivalent for the angelic side? Honest answer: no single book of equivalent stature.

The dayside material is scattered:

- Aleister Crowley's *777* (correspondence tables for the 22 paths, angels included, but a reference work, not a developed body)
- Dion Fortune's *The Mystical Qabalah* (1935), the most respected dayside synthesis, but psychological-Christian framed
- Lon Milo DuQuette's *The Chicken Qabalah* (accessible) and *Magick of Aleister Crowley* (interpretive)
- Israel Regardie's *A Garden of Pomegranates*

None of these are a body of work the way Grant's Typhonian Trilogy and *Nightside* are. The "angelic Tunnels" simply do not exist as a canonical text.

Implication: even if you wanted a balanced Tree-system, you would be assembling it from scraps. That is a sign the Tree is not the right scaffold for Subtaste.

## 4. Ifá's 256 Odù

Ifá divination, the Yoruba operational-cosmological system, uses 256 Odù (16² configurations of the Opele chain or 16 cowrie casts squared). Each Odù is a named pattern with its own character, its own divinatory verses (ese), its own ritual prescriptions, its own associated Orisha and natural domain.

The 16 primary Odù (the "majors") are: Ogbe, Oyeku, Iwori, Odi, Irosun, Owonrin, Obara, Okanran, Ogunda, Osa, Ika, Oturupon, Otura, Irete, Oshe, Ofun. Each pair of these (16 × 16 = 256) generates a unique compound configuration.

Why it beats 22 for Subtaste:

- **Richness.** 256 > 22 by an order of magnitude.
- **Operational native.** Odù are designed for combination. A primary Odù cast over a secondary Odù produces a compound; the pair generates the meaning. This is exactly what Subtaste-XOR-Subtaste needs.
- **Manifesto-aligned.** Per Section I of the Boveda Mapping Manifesto, Ifá is named explicitly: *"the 256 Odù as the oldest still-active operational-cosmological latent space."* It is the manifesto's chosen substrate.
- **Already cross-modal.** Ifá does not separate sound, story, character, ritual. It is an integrated operational language.

Limits:

- **Depth requires study.** 256 Odù is a graduate-level cosmology. Surface-level adoption produces ethnography, not method.
- **Initiation tradition.** Babalawos guard parts of the corpus. Not every use is appropriate. Public Odù vocabulary is acceptable; ritual content is not.
- **Cardinality mismatch with 12.** 256 is the substrate, not the surface relational grammar. You don't pick 12 of 256.

**Recommendation**: Ifá's 256 is the **deep substrate** for advanced relational queries (canonization decisions, era-spanning narrative arcs, multi-character narrative compositions). Not the day-to-day grammar. Surface only when the user opts into depth.

## 5. Petwo and Rada: the inherited/forged axis

Vodun (Haitian and Beninese-derived) distinguishes two spirit families:

- **Rada.** Cool, old, patient. The spirits brought across the water from Dahomey. Inherited from West Africa.
- **Petwo.** Hot, new, forged. Born in Haiti at the rupture of revolt. Coined-on-arrival.

Manifesto Article 13 names this directly:

> *"Inherited and forged are two halves of one motion. Vodun already theorizes this: Rada is the cool, old, patient spirit-family brought across the water, inherited. Petwo is the hot, new-world family born in Haiti in the furnace of revolt, forged at the rupture. A map that runs only Rada becomes ethnography; a map that runs only Petwo becomes untethered invention. Boveda mapping runs both."*

This is not a relational grammar between glyphs. It is an **orthogonal axis** that cross-cuts every Subtaste relation:

- Is the relation *inherited* (drawing on archetypal pattern, ancestor logic, returning form)?
- Is the relation *forged* (rupture-born, coined at the bridge, new-world friction)?

So the relational engine emits both:

1. The *position* (the wheel relation: tension, harmony, withdrawal, etc.)
2. The *temperature* (Rada cool, Petwo hot, or mixed)

Implementation: a single tag on each named relation. *Marronage* leans Petwo (forged at rupture). *Sankofa* leans Rada (cool inherited return). *Egbesu* leans Petwo (revolt-coded). *Mami Wata* leans Rada (ancestral water-spirit).

## 6. The Rose, not the Tree

Per Manifesto Article 6 and Section I:

> *"Tree-thinking is colonial. No single hierarchical taxonomy holds us."*

> *"Every canon work is a petal. Eras are the radii. Arcs are the concentric rings. Designations are the angles of entry. The body of work is the rose."*

The Tree of Life is a hierarchical taxonomy with a top (Kether) and bottom (Malkuth). The Rose is a radial geometry: 12 petals around a center, no top, no bottom, every petal equally proximate to the source.

For Subtaste:

- **12 glyphs = 12 petals**
- **6 relational positions** (same, sextile, square, trine, quincunx, opposition) emerge from wheel geometry. These are not borrowed from astrology; they are how circles geometrically divide. The naming convention is shared with astrology because the math is the same.
- The **angular distance** between two glyphs IS the relational position. Glyph at petal 1 vs glyph at petal 7 is opposition (180°). Glyph at petal 1 vs glyph at petal 3 is sextile (60°).
- **Inherited/forged (Rada/Petwo)** is the orthogonal axis.
- **Ifá's 256 Odù** is the deep-substrate fallback when 6 positions aren't enough.

## 7. Naming the relations

Per Manifesto Article 4: *"Coin only at bridges. Invent new words where you have built a bridge no one has crossed before. Where a good word exists, use it. Invention where none is needed is vanity."*

This means: not borrow Grant's tunnels. Not transliterate Eshu's domains literally. Coin at the user's specific four-way crossroads (Igbo × Ijaw × Jamaican × London-diasporic).

Six positions, six names. Honest first-pass candidates (the user authors the canon set; this is illustrative):

| Position | Angle | Character | Candidate name | Substrate | Temperature |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Same** | 0° | Doubling, intensification, monolith | *Mmadu* (Igbo, "the human, again") | Igbo | Rada |
| **Sextile** | 60° | Adjacent, supportive, easy resonance | *Sankofa* (Akan via Jamaica, reach-back-to-fetch) | Akan-Jamaican | Rada |
| **Square** | 90° | Friction, dynamic conflict, sharpening | *Egbesu* (Ijaw, righteous force) | Ijaw | Petwo |
| **Trine** | 120° | Harmonious flow, complementary | *Abeng* (Maroon horn, calling across distance) | Jamaican-Maroon | Rada |
| **Quincunx** | 150° | Inconjunct, awkward, unresolved kinship | *Mami Wata* (water-spirit pull, longing without resolution) | Ijaw-Niger Delta | Mixed |
| **Opposition** | 180° | Direct counter, maximum tension | *Marronage* (strategic withdrawal that founds counter-cosmology) | Jamaican-Maroon | Petwo |

These are starting candidates, not finals. The user authors the canon set, drawing on the substrate. The point: **the names are coined at the user's actual bridges**, not transliterated from Grant or imported from generic mysticism.

Where Grant's tunnels and Orisha dyads can show up: as **flavor labels per glyph-pair**, optional, never default. The same way the grading system uses Yoruba Indigo and Wuxing Charcoal for hex names without claiming the traditions. A specific OMEN ↔ JUBILATE pair might surface a flavor-label hover ("the *Gargophias* path: descent into formlessness") for users who want texture. The grammar stays Subtaste plus 6 positions plus Rada/Petwo.

## 8. Implementation map

For Tizita and the Starforge relational engine:

1. **Engine layer**: keep Starforge's existing 4 modes (collaborate, tension, growth, avoid) as the load-bearing scoring system. Already in production. Already cross-modal.
2. **Geometry layer**: compute the wheel position (1 of 6) for every glyph pair from their angular distance. Static lookup.
3. **Inheritance axis**: tag each named relation Rada / Petwo / Mixed.
4. **Surface vocabulary**: pluggable theme system.
   - **Default theme**: clean descriptive English (Tension, Harmony, Friction, Withdrawal, Continue, Forge). Commercial-legible.
   - **Cherubsphinx preset**: the user's coinages (Mmadu, Sankofa, Egbesu, Abeng, Mami Wata, Marronage). Brand-aligned.
   - **User-defined**: bring-your-own-substrate per Manifesto Section VII test. Any creator can author their own six labels.
5. **Deep-substrate query**: Ifá's 256 Odù as a higher-resolution oracle for canonization decisions. Surface only when the user opts in. Probably a specific feature, not default.
6. **Flavor layer**: optional Grant-tunnel and Orisha-dyad labels per pair, hover-only, never primary.
